Ham Bread

This recipe has more than 100 years accompanying Venezuelans at Christmas thanks to the use of representative ingredients of those holidays. Its original version is a semi-sweet bread dough filled with cooked ham and decorated with raisins and olives. Some have made updated versions with puff pastry, cream cheese, and bacon.

Ingredients

  • For the dough:
    • 1/3 cup (80 ml) milk
    • 1/3 cup (80 ml) water
    • 2 cups + 1 tablespoon bread flour (all-purpose flour will work fine too)
    • 1 tablespoon (12 g) granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon instant yeast (1 envelope - about 7 g)
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 egg
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
    • Egg wash = 1 whole egg + 1 tablespoon water
    • Melted butter for brushing after baking
  • Filling:
    • 2 cups pimiento-stuffed green olives, drained and sliced
    • 1 lb ham
    • 1 lb bacon
    • 1 1/2 cups raisins

Directions

  1. Line a baking tray with lightly greased parchment paper.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine milk and water and microwave in 30-second increments until the mixture is warmed (110°F approx.,)
  3. Add flour, sugar, yeast, and salt into the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the hook (bread paddle) attachment. Make a well in the center and add the milk mixture, egg and melted butter to the well; mix on medium speed until incorporated. Increase the speed to medium-high and mix until the dough forms a ball and pulls away from the bowl's sides, about 8 - 10 minutes. The dough should be elastic but not sticky; if too sticky, add a little more flour, about a tablespoon at a time.
  4. Place the dough on a lightly floured surface and form a ball. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm place until puffy and doubled in size, about 1-2 hours.
  5. Then, place the dough on a lightly floured surface and roll it into r 15-x-13 inch (38-x-33 cm) rectangle, about ½ inch (1.5 cm) thick.
  6. Arrange a row of whole olives on the upper edge. Slice the rest of the olives.
  7. Line the dough with the ham and bacon slices, slightly overlapping each piece and covering the dough's entire surface.
  8. Sprinkle the raisins and sliced olives on top of the ham, scattering them evenly throughout.
  9. Firmly roll it up like a Swiss roll. Transfer the roll, seam-side down, to the prepared baking sheet. Put a damp towel, loosely on top of the bread, place it in a warm and humid area in your kitchen, and let it sit for 60 minutes.
  10. Preheat oven to 350º F (180º C).
  11. Brush bread with egg wash for all sides. Bake for 40 – 45 minutes, or until golden brown. Remove the bread from the oven, and while is still hot brush with melted butter
  12. Let the bread cool for 15 – 20 minutes before serving.
